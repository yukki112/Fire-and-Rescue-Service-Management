About Us

Quezon City Fire District

The Quezon City Fire District is a modern, dynamic fire and rescue service committed to protecting the lives and properties of over 3 million residents in Quezon City.

Established in 2025, we have grown to become one of the most advanced fire departments in the Philippines, with 25 fire stations strategically located throughout the city.

Our personnel undergo continuous training in the latest firefighting and rescue techniques, and we maintain state-of-the-art equipment to ensure we're always ready to respond to emergencies.

We are committed to excellence, integrity, and bravery in serving our community since 2025.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I report a fire emergency?

In case of a fire emergency, immediately call the emergency hotline 160 or 911. Provide your exact location, the type of emergency, and any other relevant details. Stay on the line until the operator confirms your information.

How can I request a fire safety inspection?

You can request a fire safety inspection by visiting your nearest fire station or through our online portal. Businesses are required to have annual fire safety inspections to ensure compliance with fire codes.

What should I do during a fire incident?

If a fire occurs, remember to stay calm, activate the fire alarm, and evacuate immediately. Close doors behind you to contain the fire. Use stairs, not elevators. Once outside, call emergency services and provide your location.

How can I join the Quezon City Fire Department?

We regularly announce recruitment drives. Requirements include Philippine citizenship, age between 21-35 years, good physical condition, and at least 72 college units. Successful applicants undergo rigorous training before deployment.

Where can I find fire safety educational materials?

Fire safety guides and resources are available in the Educational Materials section of our website. You can download PDFs and other materials for free.

How do I request ambulance or rescue services?

Call our emergency hotlines listed on the website or dial 911 for immediate ambulance or rescue assistance. Provide clear details about your location and the nature of the emergency.

What are common causes of fire in Quezon City?

Common causes include faulty electrical wiring, unattended cooking, open flames, and improper storage of flammable materials. Regular inspections and safety practices help prevent these incidents.

How can I volunteer for fire prevention programs?

You may contact your nearest fire station or visit our website for information on community volunteer opportunities and fire safety advocacy programs.

How do I check the status of my fire safety inspection request?

You can check the status by logging into your account on our online portal or by contacting the fire station where you submitted your request.
